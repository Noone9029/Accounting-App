# Close readiness policy

## Severity

- `BLOCKER` prevents preparation, review, close, and lock. System-generated blockers are non-overridable unless a separate, existing accounting policy explicitly allows an override.
- `WARNING` requires visible acknowledgement with a safe reason when acknowledgement is available. It does not silently become complete.
- `INFORMATION` records useful context and never prevents close.
- `NOT_APPLICABLE` is excluded from completion counts.

System-generated checks are derived from their source services and cannot be manually completed. Manual checklist tasks may be assigned, completed, reopened, and evidenced without mutating accounting records.

## Current policy mapping

FX blockers remain blockers because `FxCloseReadinessService` already enforces them at the existing fiscal-period transition. Recurring readiness remains warning-level because its existing contract sets `blocksFiscalClose` to `false`; when scoped to a fiscal period, it uses each run's scheduled local date rather than the UTC scheduling instant, matching the generated accounting document's `entryDate`. Manual draft journals dated within the selected fiscal period are an authoritative `WARNING`: LedgerByte surfaces their count and latest source update for accountant review, including recurring-generated journals by their current `entryDate`, but does not auto-post or alter them. Recurring readiness does not duplicate generated journal drafts. Draft sales invoices dated within the selected fiscal period are also an authoritative `WARNING`: LedgerByte uses the existing invoice `issueDate`, keeps the source tenant-scoped, and does not auto-finalize or alter the invoice. Draft credit notes dated within the selected fiscal period are an authoritative `WARNING` using the existing credit-note `issueDate`; LedgerByte does not auto-finalize, allocate, or otherwise alter them. Unapplied customer payments are an authoritative `WARNING` only when the existing payment record is posted, non-voided, dated in the selected fiscal period, and has a positive unapplied base balance; LedgerByte does not allocate, reverse, or alter the payment. Draft purchase bills dated within the selected fiscal period are an authoritative `WARNING` using the existing bill `billDate`; LedgerByte does not auto-finalize or alter the bill. Draft purchase debit notes dated within the selected fiscal period are an authoritative `WARNING` using the existing debit-note `issueDate`; LedgerByte does not auto-finalize, allocate, or otherwise alter them. Unapplied supplier payments are an authoritative `WARNING` only when the existing payment record is posted, non-voided, dated in the selected fiscal period, and has a positive unapplied base balance; LedgerByte does not allocate, reverse, or alter the payment. Draft cash expenses dated within the selected fiscal period are an authoritative `WARNING` using the existing expense `expenseDate`; LedgerByte does not post, void, delete, or otherwise alter the expense. Unreconciled bank statement transactions dated in or before the selected fiscal period are an authoritative `WARNING` using the existing unmatched statement-row status; LedgerByte does not match, categorize, ignore, import, or otherwise alter them, and does not claim direct bank-feed coverage. Incomplete bank reconciliations are an authoritative `WARNING` when an existing draft, pending-approval, or approved session overlaps the selected fiscal period; LedgerByte does not submit, approve, close, reopen, or otherwise alter the session. Draft bank deposit batches and draft card settlements dated within the selected fiscal period are authoritative `WARNING` signals using their existing workflow dates; LedgerByte does not post, match, void, or otherwise alter them, and does not claim complete bank-feed coverage. Draft inventory adjustments dated within the selected fiscal period are an authoritative `WARNING` using the existing adjustment date; LedgerByte does not approve, post, void, or otherwise alter them. Inventory variance proposals awaiting posting are an authoritative `WARNING` when existing draft, pending-approval, or approved proposals are dated in the selected fiscal period; LedgerByte does not submit, approve, post, reverse, void, or otherwise alter them. Failed report packs are an authoritative `WARNING` when an existing failed report-pack manifest overlaps the selected fiscal period; LedgerByte does not retry, regenerate, download, or otherwise alter it. Download-blocked packs are an existing archive limitation, not a failed report-pack claim. New module checks default to `WARNING` until an existing accounting policy proves they must block. Banking and inventory signals only assert what their existing manual workflows can establish; they do not assert direct bank-feed coverage, automatic inventory posting, or complete valuation proof.

Trial Balance and Balance Sheet integrity are `BLOCKER` checks: the workspace reuses the existing report calculations for the selected period, treats either unbalanced result as blocking, and fails closed if either report cannot be evaluated. Refresh, review, close, and lock revalidation execute those report reads through the same transaction when one is active; this check does not post, reclassify, or alter accounting data.

## Review and transitions

A cycle may be prepared only with no current blockers and all required manual tasks completed. A reviewer records the exact readiness hash. Any later authoritative hash change invalidates review. Close and lock rerun the checks inside the authoritative transaction and fail closed on stale, unproven, or errored readiness. A reviewer may return an open reviewed cycle to preparation with a required reason; source drift found by the close transaction automatically commits that review invalidation and a new draft snapshot before responding with a conflict. If lock revalidation finds post-close drift, LedgerByte commits a frozen `CLOSED` snapshot, a `LOCK_BLOCKED` audit event, and a safe idempotent conflict without locking the fiscal period. Recovery uses the existing authorized fiscal-period reopen workflow first; only once the fiscal period is open may a reviewer return the closed cycle to preparation for a fresh refresh and review.

Preparer and reviewer are distinct when at least two eligible users exist. A single-user demonstration organization may use a clearly labelled single-user mode only when its explicit organization policy allows it.

Document inbox review is date-scoped only when an existing inbox item has a known document date inside the selected fiscal period. Existing `REVIEW_REQUIRED` and `EXTRACTION_FAILED` items are authoritative `WARNING` signals; LedgerByte does not extract, review, create a target, or otherwise alter them. Existing extraction-disabled items are informational: they require manual review but are not an OCR-provider or close-blocker claim. Undated inbox items are excluded because LedgerByte cannot reconstruct their fiscal-period association honestly.

Open cheque instruments are a point-in-time `WARNING` when they are currently `RECEIVED`, `ISSUED`, or `DEPOSITED` and an existing issue, received, or deposit date is on or before the selected fiscal-period end. LedgerByte does not clear, bounce, void, match, post, or otherwise alter them. This signal does not reconstruct a historical open status after a later clearance, bounce, or void, and does not claim complete bank-feed coverage.
